Pre-Internet

Explore the origins of the internet, focusing on key figures and pivotal moments that led to the development of a globally connected network.

1.1 JC Licklider

Who

Joseph Carl Robnett Licklider, commonly known as JC Licklider, was an American psychologist and computer scientist.

What

Licklider was a pioneer in computer science and conceptualized the "Intergalactic Computer Network," which laid the foundation for modern internet ideas.

Why

He envisioned computers enhancing human communication and collaboration. His ideas eventually shaped the creation of ARPANET, the precursor to the internet.

When

In the early 1960s, while at ARPA, Licklider’s work significantly influenced networked computing and time-sharing systems.

How

Through his role at ARPA, he supported and funded projects focused on networking, enabling the eventual development of ARPANET.

Example:

Read Licklider's influential paper, "Man-Computer Symbiosis," where he envisions human-computer interaction for collaborative work.

1.2 Nuclear Weapons

Who

During the Cold War, the U.S. and Soviet Union developed nuclear arsenals, influencing the need for secure communication.

What

The presence and threat of nuclear weapons led to a push for a resilient, decentralized communication system to withstand nuclear attacks.

Why

Maintaining communication and command control during nuclear strikes was essential, spurring innovation in network resilience.

When

In the 1960s, the U.S. Department of Defense prioritized creating systems capable of surviving catastrophic events.

How

This led to the development of ARPANET, which used packet-switching to reroute data through multiple pathways, ensuring communication continuity.

Example:

Read about the impact of packet-switching technology on modern networks in "A Brief History of the Internet" by the Internet Society.

1.3 ARPA (Advanced Research Project Agency)

Who

ARPA, later known as DARPA, is a U.S. government agency focused on defense-related technological advancements.

What

ARPA was instrumental in creating ARPANET, the first packet-switching network and a precursor to the modern internet.

Why

In response to the Soviet Union’s launch of Sputnik, ARPA was established to drive American innovation in technology and defense.

When

Founded in 1958, ARPA’s involvement in networking technology became prominent in the 1960s with ARPANET's development starting in 1969.

How

Through extensive funding in networking research, ARPA connected research institutions, facilitating data sharing and ultimately evolving into the global internet.

Example:

Learn more about ARPA’s contributions in "ARPANET and the Internet" from the official DARPA timeline.
